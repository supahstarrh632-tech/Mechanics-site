Pulley Systems

Understand how pulley systems work to lift heavy objects and change force direction.

A pulley system is a mechanical device that uses wheels and ropes to change the direction or magnitude of a force. Pulleys are commonly used to lift heavy objects or to change the direction of a force.

Types of Pulley Systems

  1. Single Fixed Pulley: Changes the direction of the force, but not its magnitude.
  2. Single Movable Pulley: Changes the magnitude of the force, but not its direction.
  3. Compound Pulley: A combination of fixed and movable pulleys that can change both the direction and magnitude of a force.
❮ ❯

Advantages of Pulley Systems

  • Mechanical Advantage: Makes it easier to lift heavy objects.
  • Change in Direction: Useful for lifting objects in tight spaces.
  • Reduced Effort: Requires less force to lift a load, increasing efficiency.

Worked Examples on Pulley Systems

Example 1: Single Fixed Pulley

Problem: A 50 N weight is lifted using a single fixed pulley. If the effort applied is 50 N, find the mechanical advantage.

Step 1: MA = Load / Effort = 50 N / 50 N = 1

Answer: The mechanical advantage is 1.

Example 2: Single Movable Pulley

Problem: A 100 N weight is lifted using a single movable pulley. If the effort applied is 50 N, find the mechanical advantage.

Step 1: MA = Load / Effort = 100 N / 50 N = 2

Answer: The mechanical advantage is 2.

Example 3: Compound Pulley

Problem: A compound pulley system has a mechanical advantage of 3. If the effort applied is 100 N, find the maximum weight that can be lifted.

Step 1: Load = MA × Effort = 3 × 100 N = 300 N

Answer: The maximum weight that can be lifted is 300 N.
